AugRAC: Data Augmentation for Weakly Supervised Repetitive Action Counting

Md Moniruzzaman and Zhaozheng Yin


                           

    Abstract:

In this paper, we introduce a novel weakly supervised repetitive action counting network by proposing various data augmentations, named AugRAC, for counting repetitive actions from long untrimmed videos, where action interruptions are frequent. Unlike conventional approaches that rely on precise temporal annotations of all action cycles, our approach involves training the network with a dataset that provides only total action counts as ground truth. To address the inherent challenges in repetitive action counting tasks, such as variations in action speed, variations in individualsâ€™ movement patterns, occlusion, and distinguishing between periodic and non-periodic representations, we propose a diverse range of augmentations into the AugRAC framework, including temporal interpolation, time warping, occlusion, and in-order and random shuffling. To evaluate the effectiveness of our AugRAC, we perform extensive experiments on two benchmark datasets, RepCount-A and UCFRep. The experiments show that our weakly supervised AugRAC significantly outperforms all the latest fully supervised methods on both datasets with large margins, even without using precise temporal annotations.
